CHAPTER 6

VOCABULARY

6.1 Objectives:

1. To enhance the stock of words, phrases and sentences among learners to have mastery over the language.

2. To familiarize the necessary aspects of vocabulary in order to communicate well.

3. To enhance the range of mind through vocabulary.

4. To increase knowledge of language and improving receptive and productive skills.

5. To save the space and time while writing, to reduce time and speak laconic one-word substitutes list is given ultimate aim is to speak and write impressively.

6.2 Introduction to vocabulary:

In a language we use words, phrases, clauses and sentences. So to learn a language we have to be familiar with them to use the language with ease and comfort. To learn a language, it is necessary to learn sufficient stock of words of the language. The more words you learn, the more you can communicate .it is because the range of vocabulary is the range of language. Words are tools of a language. Every word conveys meaning. Communication purpose is to convey meaning and receive meaning. While speaking or writing somewhere we falter for want of an appropriate word. So to be successful in communication we must learn new words and their meanings and their usage.

There were only some thousands of words in English but now there are over a million words in English. No writer has ever used all these words in his/her writings, even great writers like William Shakespeare and John Milton used only thousands of words. According to an estimate a native user of English uses only four to five thousand words in his daily conversation. It means if we learn five thousand words that are enough to communicate English language effectively. In this unit all aspects of important list of vocabulary is given in order to learn and use the language easily.
