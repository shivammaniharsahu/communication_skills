  • MY EUPHORIA

    • It was a very fine morning; the sunrays were striking right on my face, giving me soothing warmth on that bright winter morning. It felt as if I was waking up from a profound sleep. When I opened my eyes I found myself lying on the soft grass of a huge mountain in Austria. I stood up and as I saw the scenic beauty of the place, I couldn‘t resist reaching the state of euphoria. I could see beautiful little houses, some cattle and farms from the top of the mountain. By the side of the mountain I could see a river. The sound of the river was very soothing to the ears. The panorama was so beautiful that I feel short if words to express it. I wish I could capture that beauty in my heart forever. I closed my eyes and tried to feel the comfort of the nature. When the soft breeze touched my skin my mind was completely refreshed. It is rightly said that ―One touch of nature makes the whole world kin.‖ I tried to take with me as much memories of that place as I could.

    After viewing each and every direction from the mountain, I decided to walk down closer to those little houses and farms. As I reached down I started observing those houses and the cattle closely. The cattle were very calm. The calmness of cattle added to the beauty of the whole panorama. It felt as if an artist‘s imagination had come to life. The people were all peacefully engaged in their work.

    When I started noticing the small houses, I realised that they were all arranged in a systematic pattern. I started counting them. They were 49 in number, as I said 49 out loud, I felt someone tapping my shoulder and shouting 49 loudly in my ears, the whole scene vanished and I woke up in the class saying ―present sir‖ as an answer to my roll call.

I would not give them this as a loan. Instead I would give it to them as a gift. From one person to another to ensure we all have a basic standard of living.

    Besides all this, I would like to take a world trip just once; this cannot be done without so much of money. I would take my family also for a trip around the world. This would give all of us an idea of what the world is and how we are different from others in this same world.

    I am a simple person. That is what I would like to remain even if I become a millionaire.

  • THE MOST INSPIRING PERSON IN MY LIFE

    • The most inspiring person in my life is my father. Like others, I too read about other famous personalities but I didn‘t find anyone who had inspired me like my father. He was the second sibling in his family and had an elder brother and two younger sisters. Even though born to one of the prestigious family in his place, his parents are financially not sound. He was good in studying and was first in each class he studied. To uplift the family, his brother decided to work in Nagpur while the entire family responsibilities came to my father thus he had to quit from studies and forced him to start a typewriting institute. Both the brothers worked hard for their family. After seven years, my father then joined for b.sc in zoology. Even now he used to tell me that he secured 50 marks more than the second person one his class when I am wasting my time in TV, computer… he was so sure that he will get admission to M.Sc. in in zoology in the best college. But the postman without knowing the value of the admission letter didn‘t take it seriously and thus hand over it to my father at the last date of admission. That forced him to take admission in M.A. he successfully completed it and went M.Phil. and finally got his Ph.D. in linguistics in 1995. In between he managed to arrange the marriage for both of his sisters. He married my mother in 1995 at the age of 35. Even though having a Ph.D., due to less demand in linguistics, he just got a temporary job in the UNIVERSITY OF KERALA with a low pay scale and even now he continues in that job. Despite of these, he brought me up with all resources and guidance that no one else had guided or motivated like him. He gave complete freedom to select my future provided me with all resources. Thus the feeling when I think about my father‘s life is so inspirational.
