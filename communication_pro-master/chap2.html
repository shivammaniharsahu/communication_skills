  • 2.4 Formal and Informal dialogues

    • Formal and informal language both serves different purposes. Suitable tone, the right choice of vocabulary and the way the words are put collectively may vary between the both styles. Formal language is comparatively less personal than the informal language. The tone of informal language is more personal than formal language. Formal language is used when someone is writing for professional or any academic purposes. Formal language is sophisticated and it does not use contractions, colloquialisms or use of ‘I’ or ‘We’. Informal language is extra spontaneous and casual. It is mostly used when corresponding with friends or family this can be oral or written. It is used when writing personal emails, text messages and in some business correspondence.

    1. Informal: I don’t believe that the results are truthful.

    Formal: The results are not believed to be truthful.

    2. Informal: The research work won’t continue for next two years.

    Formal: The research work will not be continued for next two years.

    Phrasal verbs

    1. Informal: The hot air balloon was blown up for the experiment.

    Formal: The hot air balloon was inflated for the experiment.

    2. Informal: The cancer patient got over his illness.

    Formal: The cancer patient recovered from his illness.

    3. Informal: The results of group study were mixed up.

    Formal: The results of the group study were confused.

    Some Slang/Colloquialism

    1. Informal: The mob was very disorderly during the protest against the chancellor.

    Formal: The crowd was very rowdy during the protest against the chancellor.

    2. Informal: Teachers still count on students to use correct punctuation and grammar in essays.

    Formal: Teachers expect students to use correct punctuation and grammar in essays.

    First person pronouns

    1. Informal: I considered various research methods for the study.

    Formal: Various research methods were considered for the study.

    2. Informal: We believe the practice is unsustainable.

    Formal: It is believed the practice is unsustainable.

    3. Informal: During the interview I asked students about their experiences.

    Formal: During the interview students were asked about their experiences.

    INFORMATION TRANSFER CYCLE

  • 2.5 Definition

    • The information content whether formal or informal in any information system can be conveyed from one person to another person via communication or information transfer, this can be from equipment to person example computer or from person to equipment. It can be from product to person like inspection or person to person in form of conversation. The overall information organization must be such that it accomplishes the needs of both the sides.

    1. Information Need

    Information is always needed it is a process of becoming attentive and conscious about details and relevance of Information.

    2. Knowledge Creation

    Knowledge is externally produced but it is empirically grounded with the help of research and development. It can also be internally produced through development and research.

    3. Knowledge Dissemination

    It is often experienced that one-way dispersion of information leads recipient to acquire help from other irrelevant alternative sources to learn more about it and gather more information. Sometimes dissemination is also used as an instrument for interactive communication as it provides an environment for multidirectional flow of information into systems or organization.

    4. Knowledge Organization

    Knowledge organization is an organized and well arranged structure of arrangement of all knowledge and information to makes it easy for further access, identification and retrieval for different purpose in organization.

    5. Knowledge Utilization

    This aim of this process is to gain knowledge about how to use the knowledge acquired in best possible way. Knowledge utilization can be further used to explain, solve problems, for further advancements and improve the structure, maintenance, development and quality of decision-making.

    6. Knowledge Preservation

    It aims in storage and maintenance of knowledge and information for further use in future.

  • 2.6 Objectives

    • Information transfer is a kind of communicative action in which the participants are actively involved in interpreting information in either linguistic form or visual form or symbolic form which is easily understood by the participant who are seeking for information in the activity without losing its essence.

    For example, teacher reads a story loudly in the class, all the students are listening to story attentively, later teacher provides student with exercise sheets in which same story content are given in the form of summary with some points are deleted.

    The learners are then required to fill the partly blanks points in connect the story line properly, which will give same essence to the story. In this activity learners are always asked to transfer information from listening to writing. And this technique can further be used to train the learner’s overall integrated skills.

  • 2.7 Advantages of Information transfer

    • The information transfer method, when properly used, has many advantages over the conventional teaching technique. As far as the association between the teacher and learner is concerned, the student (learner) becomes an active language user and participant. The learner’s enthusiasm is also boosted to comprehend, understand and to communicate the essence in different forms. It also facilitate a language teacher in effective classroom management,

    There are many advantages of Information transfer over the traditional teaching techniques because it is based on principles of language learning, interpretation and acquisition of knowledge. The information transfer technique encourages the learner to get directly involved in informational communicative activities. All information transfer activities take place when there is information gap in participants and only to overcome this gap learner indulges themselves in the process of learning.
