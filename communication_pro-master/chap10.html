MECHANICS OF NOTEMAKING

Mechanics of Note Making:

Note making is a skill to write down appropriate key points of a written material for further use. Note making is an organized method of recording and systematizing notes than note taking. Notes are taken down quickly and hence, lack of proper planning and structure may adversely affect. Note taking is more suitable while listening to lectures, presentations or for meetings, while note making is more appropriate for a written text.

Purpose:

We commonly make notes to read a book, write an article, a research paper or to prepare an essay. You put things into your own words or abridge them as well as to emphasize the key points.

Utility:

Note making is highly constructive at work places too, as it not only helps in writing reports but also in recording the major points of previously written report. At the same time notes can be used for future use like improvising, forwarding or recalling information.

Mechanism:

When one starts with note making, the two most important aspects that one has to consider are which points should be included and which should be excluded.

Read the passage or article carefully. Give heading to passage Examine the major thought of the passage Framing of heading and subheadings based on the central theme Scrutinize how main theme is being developed. Analyze subordinate themes in the passage. Framing of subheadings. Analyze further details and other points e.g. subtitles Care about margin with indent i.e. with suitable space and number must be followed.

Steps Involved in Note Making

Step I While making notes keep the following points in mind: scan the text smartly to grasp its general idea, idea and key points. At the same time take notes for further use. Read it again, this time more carefully, to find out the development of idea, the main divisions/chapters/sections of the text and their mutual relationships. Step2

Write down the major points and the sub points in the order as they come into view in the text. Also note down core ideas and keywords used while reading or listening for effectual note-making. Get acquainted with new words, terms and language in use.

Step3

Rephrase the foremost points and sub-points into shorter phrases or may be into single words. Use schematizing example: using tables, graphic representation, Use of standard abbreviations which helps to save time. First few letters of words and phrases can be the functional abbreviations that may be easily understood later also.

Example: Volume – vol.

Especially – esp. Secretary – secy. That is – i.e.

Increase -↑ Decrease -↓

PARAPHRASING

DEFINITION

Paraphrasing is a phenomenon where in one uses a passage or text of original work into his /her words. Paraphrasing is generally shorter than the original work as they are more in condensed form. Whereas summarizing to putting one‘s own ideas and thought into one‘s own words. The terms paraphrasing and summarizing are often confusing to students. Paraphrasing and summarizing are both vital writing tools. Both of these phenomenon helps to use other writers‘ works into one‘s writing using one‘s own words.

IMPORTANCES OF PARAPHRASING ARE AS FOLLOWS.

To provide support to your own ideas To give reasons of why you agree or disagree with something To give depth to your writing To refer to something that led to your ideas

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN PARAPHRASING AND SUMMARIZING

Paraphrasing is the phenomenon of re-writing similar thoughts and ideas of others into one‘s own language without altering the meaning another writer‘s words or ideas in your own words without altering the meaning. Paraphrasing is not shortening the text but to rephrase the text without leaving anything behind. Whereas summarizing, is noting down the main themes and ideas of somebody else‘s work into your own words. The foremost difference is summary is shorter about one-third of the original text as it includes framework.

PARAPHRASE WHEN SUMMARIZE WHEN

One want to use another‘s words without plagiarism You need is to only identify the main theme You need to use words of someone without the use of quotes You just want to present an general idea of the topic The ideas of the other writer are more important than his/her style You want to simplify a complex argument Sometimes you think that some words used by writer are too difficult to understand You want to concentrate the matter which suits your requirement

TIPS ON EFFECTIVE PARAPHRASING: DOS‘ AND DONTS‘

Read the text several times until you understand the meaning clearly. Without looking at the source, jot down in points whatever you have understood from the text. Rewrite the points using your own words, but without altering the meaning. Comparison of both original and paraphrased text must be done to make sure that few similar words are taken. Always use quotation marks whenever necessary. Always Cite source which have been used.

TECHNIQUES OF PARAPHRASING CHANGE FROM A CLAUSE TO A PHRASE (OR VİCE VERSA)

After he played match, Danny took a nap. After playing, Danny took a nap.

CHANGE FROM QUOTED SPEECH TO INDIRECT SPEECH (OR VİCE VERSA)

Mrs. Lajwanti said, ―I am ready for dinner.‖

Mrs. Lajwanti said she was ready for dinner.

CHANGE FROM ACTIVE VOICE TO PASSIVE VOICE (OR VİCE VERSA)

A hotel staff will carry your luggage. Your luggage will be carried by a hotel staff.

CHANGE TO SYNONYMS

Replace the original words with words that mean the same. The horse was content with the stallion The horse was happy with the stallion.

USE AN ADVERB INSTEAD OF AN ADJECTIVE OR USE A VERB TO REPLACE A NOUN.

Chapel is an accurate programmer. Chapel programs accurately.
