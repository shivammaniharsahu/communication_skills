WRITING SKILLS

Objectives

To familiarise with different formats for different drafts. To familiarise the content for various drafts. To familiarise with different words and phrases used in different contexts. To make learners known well the unity and coherence that need to maintain for a draft. Lastly, the aim is to achieve skill to draft to any context easily.

Introduction:

The effective writing helps to grow personally and professionally. Learning different forms of writing is important such as paragraph writing, letter writing, resume writing and report writing. These forms are important and essential as they play vital role in the professional career. Every form has its own importance and place from any job attempt to the ending of the service. These variety writing skills are important in searching and settling in one’s career. If paragraph writing helps to convey the idea meaning fully and orderly the others help to convey idea meaning fully and in a required and established format.

Paragraph Writing

Introduction

A paragraph consists of several sentences and deals with a particular idea. It is writing an essay or a letter or a report. The ability to write a good paragraph determines success in written communication. Paragraphs are connected together in a piece of text so that an idea in the preceding paragraph logically leads to an idea. Paragraphs are linked together lend an overall coherence to the text. Essentials/Necessities of a paragraph:

Topic Sentence:

A topic sentence is the most important sentence; it tells what the paragraph is about. A topic sentence gives the reader insight to develop the paragraph. A good paragraph satisfies with relevant, specific and convincing information. Sometimes the topic sentence may be at the end of the paragraph and supporting sentences coming first.

Supporting sentences:

A paragraph must have specific details which include facts and examples. These help to support the main idea.

Unity:

It is important that unity of sentences which give complete sense and clear idea about the main idea. Thematic connection and logical connection are important conditions of unity. In order to achieve unity these to conditions should be fulfilled.

Variety:

In writing variety refers to the range of vocabulary and sentence structure. This is achieved by referring to the dictionary, thesaurus and works of great writers.

Style:

The style should be according to the audience and purpose. The speaker or the writer must know who are his readers, audience and for what purpose the speech is to make or write. So accordingly the style should be maintained.

Connectives and their functions:

To make writing effective and meaningful certain connectives have to be used. They are called in different names. These help to elaborate the ideas as intended by the speaker/ writer. They are used to connect words in a sentence or the connectives perform different functions. They are :-

Time relationships:

first, second , now, then, next at this time , following this, afterwards , subsequently, at this point , previously, finally, consequently, concurrently, thus, simultaneously, before this, therefore , hence, soon, eventually, ever since, after that , meanwhile, from then onwards after so much time etc.

Contrasts or differences:

Though, although, yet, still, however, nevertheless, in spite of, despite, in contrast, of course, nonetheless, rather than, but etc.

Cause:

Therefore Because caused by caused by since due to owing to

Add information:

Also Besides Furthermore Moreover In addition first, second etc.

Conclusions:

Finally Eventually Lastly to sum up to conclude

Clarifications:

Comparison: Compared to in comparison similarly like as likewise As if etc.

Result:

for this reason that is why therefore as a result consequently Repeat or rephrase information: In fact, once again, to repeat, in other words etc.

Process of Writing a Paragraph:

It can be divided into three paragraphs. They are planning, First draft and final draft. Planning: Write down the points related the topic that you would like to write about and organise grouping them. Writing the first draft: To write your first draft, use the points that the list prepared. While preparing the first draft keep in mind the important points that go into the paragraph. Final draft: Revise the ideas of the first draft, organisation and language.

Tips for Effective writing:

Use simple words. Prefer small words to big words. Be clear in your expression. Use simple sentences. Keep in mind the key point’s topic, audience and purpose. Use proper link words, organise ideas clearly and coherently. Be cautious of spelling, words, grammar and punctuation. Critique your own writing to make changes if necessary. Use a proper style that should be your own and distinct. Read thoroughly at the end and eliminate the errors identified at different levels.

Letter- Writing.

Introduction:

In the words of Samuel Johnson “A man’s letters are only the mirror of life”. In fact it is right because when we read a letter, we understand of its writer but also look into his soul. Correspondence with friends and relatives gives lot of pleasure. A letter conveys mental image of a friend or a relative to the receiver of the letter. To make friends and keep alive love and affection, letters help us. Through these letters, friends and family members living in distant places are brought together. To continue our relations, to convey our problems, the routine activities of life, these letters help us. Letter writing is the most effective communication skill. They are powerful weapons in the hands of man. Our first prime minister’s letters to his daughter Indira Gandhi gained so much fame and a great deal of reputation in the minds of Indians. We can be successful in reaching our goals through writing letters properly and skilfully. Whether in the social life or in the business or offices success lies in good letters as they convey our view clearly and convincingly.

Important tips for writing letters:

Correct spelling should be used.

Use punctuation marks at the right place. Letters should be like as if you are talking to the persons concerned face to face. Write letters clearly and correctly in easy understanding way. The official letters should be written in a formal way. The business letters should be short, concise and to the point. Maintain clarity and simplicity. The beginning of the letter should be in a suitable manner. The letter should be average in length. Letters should be interesting and impressive to the readers.

Types of Letters:

Letters are divided into mainly two types. They are personal letters and official letters or formal letters. Social letters such as letters to friends, family members and relatives come under personal letters. Business letters and Application letters come under formal letters or official letters.

Parts of a letter:

There are six parts in a letter, they are:
The writer’s address or heading.
The date.
The greeting /salutation.
The content or body of the letter.
The ending /subscription.
The signature.

Resume writing

Introduction:

A well written, appropriate CV (British English) or resume (American English) is important to get to the interview stage for a job. A resume is a document containing a summary or the details of experience and educational qualifications, usually prepare for securing a job or admission into an academy. Your resume must present your skills and experience and emphasize your suitability for the job. It is based on facts and should be well presented. If your resume is poorly presented or badly written, you’re going to have trouble getting the job you expect – or even an interview. Taking the time to work on your resume is really important.

Purpose of a resume:

That you are employable. To meet the organization’s requirements  To show you have the right qualifications and education for the job.  You have the experience and the skills to obtain the job you applied for.  That you have the right level of professionalism for the job.

Resume Order:

Objective Key skills Experience Educational qualifications Personal profile. References/referees

Resume includes:

Contact details

Write your name, email address and a contact phone number on your resume.

Opening statement

An opening statement is a summary of who you are, where you've studied and/or worked, and what you bring to the job.

Technical skills:

A short list of the names of software or technology you know how to use.

Examples might include: Word processing or spreadsheet software Programming languages

Tools

Personal attributes

If no experience, a list of personal attributes is a way to demonstrate that you're the right person for the job.

Educational history

Your Educational History is to show your highest level of education. Use a few bullet points listing your academic achievements.

Employment history

Your employment history, start with the most recent job and go backwards from there. Mention the significant contributions you made to the organization. Work experience at school. Work placements or internships at university.

Volunteer work.

References:

Mention two people who can positively recommend you as an employee.

Testimonials:

It is as easy as asking a colleague, teacher or previous employer to write a couple of sentences about you. You can include any testimonials you get as part of your educational history.

Conclusion:

A resume is a document that shows experience, profile, and skills. The purpose of writing is it to get invited to job interviews. Ultimately, putting it together is an important step to getting a great job.

Tips:

Adapt your resume so that it is appropriate for the job you are applying for. Your resume does not exceed a page or two. Keep the important and impressive sections closer to the top of the page. Use Simple English and avoid professional jargon. Avoid using different font types and sizes. Send a covering letter with the resume. Lastly check your resume and covering letter carefully for errors.

REPORT WRITING

Introduction

Report writing is a formal style of writing elaborately on a topic. The tone of a report should be formal. The important thing is that the focus should be on the target audience .A report presents information on an event, situation or process. It should be written in clear, simple language and in a direct way so as to understand quickly and easily. While writing a report three things should be kept in mind. They are what the report is about, it is meant for whom, and it is used for what. Reports are of three kinds. They are news paper reports, scientific experiment process reports and official reports. News paper reports cover current events and everyday incidents of the country and the world. The subjects include education, entertainment, commerce and industry, politics, sports, science and stories of human interest. The writer of these reports present facts as objectively as possible, without expressing their own reactions to the incidents. News paper reports have four important features.

In the headings the verb is either in the simple present tense, past participle or to + infinitive. Mixture of active passive voice is used for factual descriptions. It includes the opinions of important persons and experts. News paper reports use the expressions confirmed/unconfirmed and reliable sources.

Note: The news paper reports or written for college magazine or a class assignment consists of more than one paragraph. In such reports it begins with an introduction where venue, date and time of the event is given. The next two or three paragraphs are used to describe the event in detail. The last paragraph concludes the report.

Reports of Experiments and Processes:

The reports of scientific experiments are focused on the procedure rather than the performer. The verb appears in passive voice in the entire report. Imperative sentences are used to explain the experiments in laboratories and for the demonstration of processes.

Official Reports

These reports are written in response to instructions received from the authorities. They are also called survey reports as the writer of the report study a situation or investigate on the matter before submitting the report. The reports should be full of facts, clear and concise as further decisions and action based on the report submitted.


Format of the Report:
From address.
Date.
To address.
Title of the report.
Reference.
Abstract or summary.
Body of the report.
Conclusions.
Signature of the writer.
